Reforestation Saga uses a level-based scoring system where your highest reached level serves as your score. The game tracks your progress and challenges you to continuously improve.

## Level as Score

Unlike traditional games with point systems, your score in Reforestation Saga is simply the highest level you've reached:

<Info>
Each successfully completed level increases your score by 1. There's no maximum—you can continue improving indefinitely.
</Info>

## Progress Bar

A visual progress bar in the top-left corner shows how many trees you've planted versus how many are required:

```swift
BarView(
    treesRemaining: treesRemaining,
    totalPollution: treesNeeded
)
```

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trees Remaining">
    The bar updates in real-time as you plant each tree, showing your progress toward level completion.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Visual Feedback">
    Each successful tree plant decreases the remaining count, giving immediate feedback on your advancement.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Level Context">
    The total required trees increases with each level, making the progress bar more challenging to fill.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## High Score System

### Automatic Saving

Your highest level is automatically saved to your device:

```swift
if currentLevel - 1 > gameData.highestLevel {
    gameData.highestLevel = currentLevel - 1
    UserDefaults.standard.savedHighestLevel = gameData.highestLevel
}
```

<Note>
High scores are saved immediately when you surpass your previous record, ensuring no progress is lost even if the app closes unexpectedly.
</Note>

### Persistent Storage

High scores persist across app sessions using UserDefaults:

```swift
var savedHighestLevel: Int {
    get { integer(forKey: Keys.highestLevel) }
    set { set(newValue, forKey: Keys.highestLevel) }
}
```

Your achievement is preserved permanently unless you manually reset the app data.

## New High Score Detection

When you fail a level, the game checks if you've achieved a new high score:

```swift
MissionFailedPopUp(
    currentLevel: $currentLevel,
    reason: $reason,
    isNewHighScore: currentLevel - 1 >= gameData.highestLevel,
    onNextMission: { ... }
)
```

<Accordion title="High Score Feedback">
If you've reached a new personal best:
- The mission failed popup displays special congratulations
- Your new highest level is highlighted
- The achievement is immediately saved

This recognition motivates you to keep pushing further even after failure.
</Accordion>

## Level Tracking

### Current Level Display

The current level you're playing is always visible in the top-right corner:

```swift
LevelSoundView(
    isMuted: $isMuted,
    currentLevel: currentLevel,
    scene: scene
)
```

<Info>
This helps you track in real-time whether you're approaching or surpassing your previous best.
</Info>

### Level Progression Logic

Levels increment sequentially after each successful completion:

```swift
showLevelClearPopup = false
currentLevel += 1
setupScene()
```

There's no skipping levels—you must complete each one in order to progress.

## Failure and Reset

When you fail a level, the game resets to level 1:

```swift
onNextMission: {
    currentLevel = 1
    gameData.highestLevel = UserDefaults.standard.savedHighestLevel
    showMissionFailedPopup = false
    setupScene()
}
```

<Warning>
Even though you restart from level 1, your highest level record remains intact. The restart creates a fresh challenge to beat your previous score.
</Warning>

## Competitive Elements

### Personal Best Challenge

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Your Record">
    Your first playthrough establishes your baseline high score.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Compete With Yourself">
    Each subsequent run challenges you to surpass your previous achievement.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Infinite Improvement">
    With no level cap, there's always room to improve and set new records.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Achievement Milestones

Certain level thresholds represent significant achievements:

<Accordion title="Milestone Levels">
- **Level 5**: Complete the tutorial phase with consistent rotation
- **Level 10**: Master variable rotation directions  
- **Level 15**: Demonstrate expert timing with random rotation speeds
- **Level 20+**: Enter elite territory with maximum difficulty

Each milestone marks your progression from beginner to expert player.
</Accordion>

## Score Improvement Strategies

<Tip>
To maximize your high score, focus on consistency rather than speed. Careful blink timing prevents failures that reset your progress.
</Tip>

### Analyzing Failures

The game provides specific failure feedback:

```swift
let soundEffectName: String = reason == "laut" ? "water" : "failed"
```

<Steps>
  <Step title="Water Failure">
    If you plant in water, a splash effect and specific sound help you recognize the mistake.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Collision Failure">
    If you hit an existing tree, a crash effect indicates the collision point.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Learn Patterns">
    Understanding why you failed helps you avoid the same mistake in future attempts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## No External Leaderboards

<Note>
Reforestation Saga focuses on personal achievement rather than global competition. Your high score is private and stored only on your device.
</Note>

This design encourages you to enjoy the meditative gameplay without pressure from external comparisons.

## Tracking Progress Over Time

Since high scores persist across sessions, you can:

- Return to the game weeks later and see your previous best
- Set goals for specific level targets (e.g., "reach level 15")
- Track gradual improvement as you learn optimal timing strategies
- Challenge friends informally by comparing highest levels

## Audio Feedback

Success and failure are reinforced through sound:

```swift
GameMusicManager.shared.playSoundEffect(filename: "success")
```

<Accordion title="Sound Effects">
- **Tree Planted**: Satisfying confirmation sound when trees successfully attach
- **Level Complete**: Celebration sound when finishing a level
- **Water Splash**: Distinctive audio when planting in forbidden zones  
- **Collision**: Impact sound when hitting existing trees

These audio cues provide immediate feedback on your scoring performance without requiring you to look at UI elements.
</Accordion>

## Motivation System

<Info>
The combination of immediate feedback (progress bar), session goals (complete current level), and long-term achievement (high score) creates a compelling motivation loop that encourages continued play.
</Info>

Each element works together to make every blink meaningful and every level completion rewarding.
